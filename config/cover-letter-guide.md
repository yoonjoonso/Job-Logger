# Cover Letter Style Guide

Derived from real cover letter usage patterns in this repo.
Use this as the tone, structure, and rule reference before generating any cover letter.

---

## Structure (always 3–5 paragraphs, ~250–380 words)

### Opening line
Always: `Dear [Company] Hiring Team,`
Second line: `I'm applying for the [exact role title] role.` — OR integrate into a one-sentence opener.

### Paragraph 1 — Who I am (1–2 sentences)
State experience level and most relevant framing for this role. Do NOT use "I'm passionate about" or "I'm excited to."

**Examples by archetype:**
- Gameplay: "I have 7+ years shipping games in Unity across PC, console, and VR..."
- Backend: "I have 7+ years building backend systems for live-service games in distributed environments..."
- General SWE: "I have 7+ years of professional experience in C# across multiple shipped titles, working through the full software development lifecycle..."
- Lead: "I have 7+ years of professional experience in Unity and C#, shipping cross-platform and live-service titles... with hands-on experience building and maintaining client systems in large, evolving codebases."

### Paragraph 2–3 — Proof points (the body, 2–4 specific bullets woven into prose)
Map the candidate's real work directly to what the JD is asking for. Pull from `cv.md` or the approved structured source and never invent metrics.

**Priority proof points by archetype:**

Fill this table with your own proof points from cv.md. Examples of what to include per archetype:

| Archetype | Lead with | Also use |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Backend game / Live-ops | [your live-service backend system] | [your cloud stack and services] |
| Gameplay | [your shipped gameplay systems] | [your other shipped titles or tech lead roles] |
| Lead / Tech lead | [your tech lead experiences] | [cross-functional or studio-wide ownership] |
| Platform / SDK | [your platform certification work] | [your multi-platform titles] |
| VR/XR | [your VR titles and headsets] | [simulation or training projects] |
| SWE / CI-CD | [your CI/CD and testing work] | [your end-to-end ownership examples] |
| Systems | [your systems-level work] | [your data stores and architecture] |

### Paragraph 4 (optional) — Gap acknowledgment
When there are real gaps (C++, mobile, specific tech), acknowledge them **briefly and with confidence**. One sentence per gap, two gaps maximum. Lead with what you have, land on the fast-ramp.

**Rules:**
- Never over-explain. Don't say "the patterns are the same, it's just the APIs" — that reads as defensive.
- Never use negative framing without a positive end: "not something I'd claim as ready today" is a bad close. End on what you CAN do, not what you can't.
- Don't say "require a ramp" — say "something I pick up quickly" or "actively building on."
- One honest sentence is enough. If you find yourself writing two sentences about the same gap, cut one.

**Examples:**
- GCP/Azure gap: "My cloud background is Azure-native; GCP is something I pick up quickly."
- C++ gap: "My Unreal experience is Blueprint-based — C++ plugin authoring is something I'm actively building toward."
- Mobile gap: "I haven't shipped a mobile title, but my F2P and live-service backend patterns transfer directly."
- Never apologize for game dev background — frame it as scale and ownership evidence.

### Paragraph 5 (optional) — Personal connection
**Only include if GENUINE.** Examples of the kind of connection worth including:
- Studio you've followed since a specific title you played
- Game or franchise that's been part of your life
- Community or esports involvement related to their games

If there's no genuine connection: **skip this paragraph entirely.** Do not manufacture one.

### Closing (2 sentences max)
- "Thank you for your time and consideration." (standard)
- Or: "I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss further. Thank you for your time."
- NEVER: "I look forward to hearing from you," "I'm excited for the opportunity," "Please consider my application"
- Sign-off: `Sincerely, [Candidate Name]` OR just `[Candidate Name]`

---

## Tone Rules (non-negotiable)

1. **Direct, concrete, no fluff.** If a sentence doesn't add a proof point or frame the candidate, cut it.
2. **Quantify naturally.** Use numbers only if they already exist in the approved source material.
3. **Honest about gaps, not apologetic.** Gaps get one sentence, never a paragraph.
4. **Personal connection is earned, not performed.** Skip it if it's not real.
5. **No passive voice.** "I built" not "was responsible for building."
6. **No corporate-speak.** No "leveraged," "synergy," "passionate about," "unique opportunity."
7. **The hook is the proof, not the claim.** "I built a ranked system with server-authoritative validation" not "I'm great at backend systems."
8. **3–5 paragraphs. ~250–380 words. One page.**
9. **NEVER use em-dashes (—).** Hard rule, no exceptions. Rewrite the sentence instead.
10. **No 3-item rhythm cadence.** Avoid AI-tell tricolon patterns like "X, Y, and Z" stacked in series. If you find yourself listing three things in a row, restructure. Use two items or break into separate sentences.

---

## What NOT to do (from writing these letters)

- Don't list every proof point — pick 2–3 that directly map to the specific JD
- Don't address every gap — only the major ones that are clearly required
- Don't re-summarize the resume — tell the story the resume can't
- Don't start with "I am writing to express my interest in..."
- Don't end with "I look forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience"
